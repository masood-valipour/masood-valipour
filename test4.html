01 · Background

Why entangled photons?

Classical two-photon absorption (TPA) requires high peak intensities, typically from pulsed lasers. Entangled photon pairs can in principle achieve the same nonlinear response at drastically lower photon fluxes — a consequence of photon-pair correlations that bypass the quadratic intensity dependence of classical TPA.

Classical TPA

Absorption probability scales as I². Requires ultrashort, high-energy pulses. Photons are statistically independent.

Entangled TPA (ETPA)

Absorption can scale linearly with flux under ideal conditions. Time–energy entanglement encodes correlations that match molecular resonances.

ETPA rate (leading order)
RETPA ∝ |∫∫ dω₁ dω₂ · f(ω₁,ω₂) · M(ω₁,ω₂)|²

where f(ω₁,ω₂) is the joint spectral amplitude of the entangled pair and M(ω₁,ω₂) is the two-photon transition matrix element of the target molecule.

04 · Result

Temporal correlations & entanglement time

Time–energy entangled pairs have a well-defined entanglement time Te. When Te matches the dephasing timescale of the molecular transition, ETPA is enhanced. The plot traces ETPA rate vs. Te for a model two-level system.

Fig. 3 — Normalized ETPA rate vs. entanglement time Te (sample curve)

Peak ETPA occurs when Te ≈ T₂ (molecular dephasing time, dashed line). For Te ≫ T₂ the rate decays as 1/Te, recovering classical behavior. This is a sample trace — real molecular parameters will replace it.

05 · Methods

Computational approach

The optimization combines analytical gradients derived from the ETPA perturbation theory kernel with numerical evaluation of the joint spectral amplitude over a discretized frequency grid.

Spectral grid

512 × 512 frequency points centered at degeneracy. Evaluated using FFT-based convolution for the molecular response integral.

Optimizer

L-BFGS-B with analytic Jacobian. Convergence within 80–120 iterations. Multiple random restarts to probe non-convex landscape.

Molecular model

Sum-over-states expansion truncated at the three lowest excited states. Transition dipoles from TD-DFT (B3LYP/6-31G*).

Entanglement characterization

Schmidt decomposition of the JSA. Entanglement entropy and Schmidt number K computed for each optimized source.
